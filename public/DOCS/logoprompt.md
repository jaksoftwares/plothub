Design a world-class technology brand identity for "PlotHub."

PlotHub is not a real estate listing website. It is an enterprise-grade global platform that digitizes the entire land commerce ecosystem, enabling buyers, vendors, developers, investors, and organizations to discover, manage, market, and transact land with trust, transparency, and modern technology.

The identity should position PlotHub as a premium technology company rather than a traditional property business.

Create a timeless, minimal, geometric logo inspired by the design philosophy of Stripe, Linear, Tesla, Notion, OpenAI, Airbnb, Vercel, and Figma.

The logo must be highly recognizable, scalable, and memorable while remaining extremely simple.

Avoid literal representations of land.

Do NOT use houses, rooftops, buildings, location pins, maps, trees, mountains, keys, shields, globes, handshake symbols, parcel outlines, or any cliché real estate imagery.

Instead, explore abstract geometric forms inspired by:

• connection
• trust
• structure
• precision
• coordinates
• digital infrastructure
• foundation
• networks
• ownership
• alignment
• intelligent systems
• modular architecture
• growth

The symbol should be capable of becoming iconic even when viewed without the company name.

Use clean geometric construction, balanced proportions, generous whitespace, consistent stroke weights, and optical alignment.

The accompanying wordmark should use a custom geometric sans-serif style with excellent readability, premium spacing, and subtle character modifications that make it unique without sacrificing clarity.

Use a restrained color palette:

Primary Forest:
#1F4D3D

Dark Forest:
#173A2E

Accent Gold:
#C48A2C

Charcoal:
#111827

White:
#FFFFFF

The logo must work equally well in:

• full color
• monochrome black
• monochrome white
• embossed
• engraved
• favicon
• mobile app icon
• website header
• business cards
• legal documents
• construction site signage
• social media
• merchandise

The final result should feel like a billion-dollar technology company, not a property agency.
