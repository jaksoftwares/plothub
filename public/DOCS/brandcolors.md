For PlotHub, you're not selling excitement; you're selling trust, confidence, permanence, and investment. Buying land is often one of the biggest financial decisions a person will make, so the interface should feel calm, stable, and credible.

I would avoid the common real estate palette of bright greens and blues. It's overused and often makes platforms feel generic.

Color Philosophy

Think of the interface as 95% neutral.

Color should communicate state, action, and hierarchy—not decorate the UI.

Most successful SaaS products (Linear, Notion, Vercel, Stripe) rely heavily on neutrals, using brand color sparingly for emphasis.

How the Colors Work Together

Imagine the homepage:

White background
Dark charcoal text
Forest green primary buttons
Soft green sections for trust content
Amber highlights for premium properties
Slate icons and dividers
Minimal use of blue (system information only)

This creates a calm, premium experience where content—not color—takes center stage.

Color Usage Ratio

A useful guideline is:

70% Neutral whites and light grays
20% Charcoal and slate for typography and structure
8% Deep forest green for primary actions and brand identity
2% Amber for emphasis and premium moments

This restraint helps the interface feel timeless.
