I would actually not start by asking AI to "design a logo."

The mistake most startups make is prompting:

"Create a modern logo for PlotHub."

That almost always produces:

Houses
Roofs
Buildings
Location pins
Trees
Maps
Generic geometric icons
Overused real estate symbols

None of those become iconic brands.

Look at the companies you admire:

Tesla → Stylized "T" (not a car)
SpaceX → Typography with a subtle orbital stroke
Stripe → Pure typography
Linear → Pure typography
Notion → Simple geometric cube
Figma → Abstract geometric mark
OpenAI → Abstract symbol
Airbnb → Bélo symbol
Dropbox → Abstract open box

None of them literally draw what they sell.

Design Philosophy

The logo should represent the platform, not the product.

We are building infrastructure.

Trust.

Connection.

Ownership.

Technology.

Professionalism.

The symbol should communicate these values rather than depict land or houses.

One Strategic Suggestion

If your long-term ambition is to build something on the scale of Stripe, Tesla, or SpaceX, I would consider not investing heavily in a logo until the final brand name is settled.

A comprehensive brand system—logo, wordmark, iconography, favicon, typography, and motion identity—is a significant investment. If "PlotHub" is a working title, it's worth finalizing the permanent name first. That way, every asset you create contributes directly to the enduring brand rather than needing to be recreated later. The design philosophy above will remain valid regardless of the eventual name, but the logo itself is best built around the final brand identity.
